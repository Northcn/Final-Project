Guinea Pigs cannot live alone as they are social creatures and will get depressed. They must live in pairs or more.

Hay makes up 80% of their diet

Female guinea pigs are referred as sows and male guinea pigs are referred as boars

Boars are much harder to bond then sows because they are much more territorial, so it's recommended to only keep 2 boars together
But you can usually have more then 2 sows bonded and living together.

Guinea Pigs wheek for many reasons like food, excitement, or you did something that made them happy.

Happy Guinea Pigs "popcorn". Like corn kennels popping, Guinea Pigs also jump into the air unexpectedly when their happy.

They can hear way better than humans. So if you live in a noisy environment, Guinea Pigs aren't the right pets.

Some breeds need special care like Hairless Guinea Pigs need a healthy diet to keep their skin healthy, and whenever they have dry skin you must apply coconut or almond oil
You must also have a comfortable room temperature and have extra heating sources since they have no fur to keep them warm

Long-haired breeds need regular grooming and sometimes help cleaning themselves since their fur is more likely to get dirty

Guinea Pigs need nail trims once every month, or if you prefer every 2-3 weeks.

Guinea Pigs are considered EXOTIC PETS so you cannot take them to just any vet, it must be an exotic vetenarian which can be difficult to find or a far drive
And an exotic vet is usually more expensive than a regular vet
